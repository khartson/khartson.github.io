My name is Kyle Hartson. I am from Cleveland, Ohio currently working as an Software Developer at Eurofins. 
My current work focuses on the development and maintenance of software systems that support our laboratory 
operations, as well as the services with which our customers integrate and interact. Our team works across the 
stack, managing DevOps, development, and support tasks, with our primary technologies being: 

- **Development**: C#/.NET 10, TypeScript/React, VB6/ASP Classic (supported legacy systems)
- **Infrastructure**: Azure, Kubernetes (Azure Kubernetes Services), Azure Pipelines, Azure Repos, Azure Application Gateways, Azure SQL (MSSQL), Azure VMs
- **Databases**: MSSQL 
- **Development environment**: Windows, Linux

I am also on the team at [Digital Graphene](https://www.digitalgraphene.com), a Cleveland-based group where I work alongside 3 highly-experienced colleagues 
in the contracted development and digital solutions space. Our flagship product is [TrackSurfer](https://www.tracksurfer.io), a production tool for 
narrative audio creators and teams, built on Microsoft Azure's serverless platforms. 

## My Interests

### DevOps/DevSecOps 

Having started my career on a helpdesk, I take special interest in the intersection of Software Development, IT Infrastructure, and Cybersecurity.
Most of my pet projects of personal interests center on: 

- The integration and development of _shift-left_ strategies into the infrastructure that supports the development lifecycle 
- Understanding not only the current paradigms of cloud computing _but also_ the networking and administrative tenents that underpin these abstraction layers
- Learning and implementing the tools to harden and secure software systems, from SAST/DAST to software-defined mesh networking 
- Staying curious and informed on issues of software security to develop the skillset to be reactive to a burgeoning technical landscape

### Software Development 

In software development, even more than writing code, I find that my interests keenly align with: 

- How to continually improve/optimize the developer experience (DX) 
  - This can entail solutions ranging from generative tools/automations to learning 
    theories/paradigms that drive adaptive, effective development 
- Ways of automating, enhancing, and standardizing the discipline of software development via documentation and process frameworks 
- Learning when and how to balance development priorities, such as when to reuse existing solutions versus developing a novel approach
- Understanding fundamentals of software composition/architecture and why such systems and patterns exist

  - Various LXC/containers for hosting scripts, tools
